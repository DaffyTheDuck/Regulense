SECTION 1: CUSTOMER ONBOARDING
===========================================================

1.1 Identity Verification
All customers must provide:
- Government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License, or Voter ID)
- Recent passport-size photograph
- Address proof (utility bill, bank statement, or rent agreement)

Note: PAN card is collected on a best-effort basis. It is currently MANDATORY only for transactions exceeding INR 2,00,000 in aggregate per year.

1.2 Digital Verification Process
We use the following for customer verification:
- Aadhaar-based OTP verification
- DigiLocker for document authentication
- Live photograph capture with location tagging
- Video KYC for loan customers above INR 50,000

1.3 Age and Eligibility
Customers must be at least 18 years old. We verify age through government-issued ID documents.

SECTION 2: BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP
===========================================================

2.1 Corporate Customer Requirements
For business and corporate customers, we collect:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- PAN of the company
- GST Registration Certificate
- List of Directors with their ID proofs
- Latest financial statements (if available)

2.2 Beneficial Ownership Declaration
We collect beneficial ownership information to the best extent possible based on customer declarations. For complex corporate structures, we document the ownership structure as provided by the customer.

Note: We are developing enhanced procedures for beneficial ownership verification as per latest guidelines.

===========================================================
SECTION 3: TRANSACTION MONITORING
===========================================================

3.1 Manual Review Process
Our operations team reviews daily transaction reports every morning at 9:00 AM. Weekly summaries of high-value transactions are prepared for management review.

3.2 Transaction Limits
Current system limits:
- Per transaction: INR 1,00,000
- Daily limit: INR 2,00,000
- Monthly limit: INR 10,00,000

3.3 Cash Transaction Monitoring
Cash deposits through partner agents:
- Cash deposits above INR 50,000 require Branch Manager approval
- We maintain daily cash transaction registers
- Monthly summary prepared for compliance review

Note: We are working on implementing the RBI threshold of INR 10,00,000 for Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR). Currently, our system captures all cash transactions above INR 50,000 for internal monitoring purposes.

3.4 Suspicious Activity Detection
Transactions are flagged for review if they:
- Are inconsistent with customer's known profile
- Show frequent credits and immediate withdrawals
- Involve newly opened accounts with high activity
- Come from customers who are evasive about transaction purpose

3.5 Alert Management
When our system generates an alert:
- Operations team reviews within 24 hours
- If suspicious, escalated to Compliance Officer
- Compliance Officer investigates within 48 hours
- Decision made to close alert or file STR

===========================================================
SECTION 4: REPORTING PROCEDURES
===========================================================

4.1 Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR)
STRs are filed when the Compliance Officer concludes a transaction is suspicious.

Filing Timeline:
- Investigation completed within 7 days of alert
- STR filed with FIU-IND within 7 days of conclusion
- Total timeline target: 14 days from initial alert

Note: We are working to streamline this to meet RBI's 7-day filing requirement from the point of forming suspicion.

4.2 Confidentiality
STR filing is STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL. Customer is never informed. All employees sign annual confidentiality undertakings.

4.3 Cash Transaction Reports
We currently file CTRs for transactions exceeding our internal threshold of INR 2,00,000. System upgrade is planned to align with RBI's INR 10,00,000 threshold.

Monthly compilation is done by Finance team, reviewed by Compliance Officer, and submitted to FIU-IND by 20th of following month.

===========================================================
SECTION 5: RECORD KEEPING
===========================================================

5.1 Document Retention Policy
We maintain records for minimum 5 years from account closure:
- KYC documents (ID proof, address proof, photographs)
- Account opening forms
- Transaction records
- Correspondence with customers

Storage: Physical files for pre-2020 customers; Digital scans for 2020 onwards.

Note: Historical records from 2015-2019 are being migrated to long-term storage to meet the 5-year requirement.

5.2 Data Security
Customer records are protected through:
- Encrypted storage (AES-256)
- Role-based access controls
- Weekly backups
- Annual security audits

5.3 Record Retrieval
Digital records: Within 2 hours
Physical records: Within 24 hours

SECTION 6: TRAINING AND AWARENESS
===========================================================

6.1 Employee Training
All new employees receive 2-hour AML/KYC training during onboarding covering:
- Overview of PMLA and RBI regulations
- Company policies and procedures
- Red flags and suspicious indicators

6.2 Annual Refresher Training
All employees complete 1-hour annual refresher training through our online learning portal.

Completion Target: 100% by December 31
Current Status (as of June 2023): 87% completion

Note: We are enhancing our training program to meet the RBI requirement of minimum 4 hours annually for all staff.

6.3 Compliance Team Training
Compliance officers attend external certification programs and workshops on AML/CFT best practices.

===========================================================
SECTION 7: RISK ASSESSMENT
===========================================================

7.1 Customer Risk Categories
We categorize customers as:
- STANDARD RISK (majority of retail customers)
- HIGH RISK (self-employed, high transaction volumes, cash-intensive businesses)

Note: We are developing a "Low Risk" category for salaried employees from listed companies and government departments.

7.2 High-Risk Customer Controls
For high-risk customers:
- Branch Manager approval required for account opening
- Quarterly account review by compliance team
- Enhanced transaction monitoring

7.3 PEP Screening
Currently, we rely on customer self-declaration for PEP identification. We are evaluating automated PEP screening tools for implementation in Q4 2023.
