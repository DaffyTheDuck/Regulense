RBI MASTER DIRECTION - KYC AND AML COMPLIANCE 2024

SECTION 1: CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION

1.1 PAN Card Requirement
All customers must provide Permanent Account Number (PAN) card for transactions exceeding INR 50,000. This is MANDATORY and non-negotiable for compliance with Income Tax regulations.

1.2 Customer Due Diligence
Regulated entities shall undertake Customer Due Diligence (CDD) measures when:
- Opening new accounts or commencing business relationships
- Carrying out occasional transactions exceeding INR 10,00,000 (Ten Lakh)
- When there is suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing
- When there is doubt about previously obtained customer identification data

1.3 Address Verification
Current address proof not older than 2 months must be obtained from all customers. Acceptable documents include utility bills, bank statements, or government-issued address proof.

1.4 Photograph Requirement
Recent passport-size photographs must be obtained for all individual customers during account opening.

SECTION 2: BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP

2.1 Identification Requirement - Companies
For corporate entities, beneficial owners are natural persons who ultimately own or control 25% or more of shares or voting rights. All such beneficial owners must be identified and verified.

2.2 Identification Requirement - Partnerships
For partnership firms, beneficial owners are partners who own or control 15% or more capital contribution.

2.3 Documentation
Complete beneficial ownership structure must be documented, verified, and maintained. This includes ownership charts, shareholder registers, and trust deeds where applicable.

2.4 Verification Standards
Beneficial ownership information must be verified through independent sources such as company registries, financial statements, or third-party databases.

SECTION 3: TRANSACTION MONITORING

3.1 Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR)
All cash transactions (deposits or withdrawals) exceeding INR 10,00,000 (Ten Lakh) must be reported to Financial Intelligence Unit - India (FIU-IND) within 15 days from the end of the month.

3.2 Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)
Suspicious transactions must be reported to FIU-IND within 7 days of forming suspicion, regardless of the transaction amount. The fact of filing STR must be kept strictly confidential.

3.3 Transaction Monitoring System
Automated transaction monitoring systems must be implemented to identify:
- Unusual patterns of transactions
- Transactions not in line with customer profile
- Structuring of transactions to avoid reporting thresholds
- Sudden increases in transaction volumes

3.4 High-Risk Transactions
Enhanced monitoring is required for:
- Transactions with high-risk countries
- Large cash transactions
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Non-face-to-face customers

SECTION 4: RECORD KEEPING

4.1 Retention Period
All KYC documents, account opening forms, and transaction records must be maintained for a minimum period of 5 years from the date of account closure or cessation of transaction.

4.2 Transaction Records
Complete details of all transactions must be preserved for 5 years to enable reconstruction of individual transactions if required by authorities.

4.3 Data Security
Customer information and transaction records must be stored securely with:
- Encryption for electronic records
- Access controls and audit trails
- Regular backups and disaster recovery procedures

4.4 Retrieval Requirements
All records must be readily retrievable and producible to regulatory authorities within 24 hours of request.

SECTION 5: TRAINING AND AWARENESS

5.1 Employee Training - Mandatory
All employees must receive minimum 4 hours of AML/CFT training annually. Training must cover:
- Relevant laws and regulations (PMLA, UAPA)
- Institution's AML/CFT policies
- Red flags and suspicious transaction indicators
- Reporting procedures

5.2 Compliance Officer Training
The Principal Officer and compliance team must receive specialized training on:
- Investigation techniques
- STR/CTR filing procedures
- Interface with law enforcement
- Latest money laundering typologies

5.3 Training Documentation
Attendance records, training materials, and completion certificates must be maintained for regulatory review.

SECTION 6: RISK CATEGORIZATION

6.1 Customer Risk Assessment
All customers must be categorized as Low, Medium, or High risk based on:
- Customer profile and occupation
- Nature and location of business
- Countries of operation
- Transaction patterns

6.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
High-risk customers require:
- Senior management approval before account opening
- Source of wealth and source of funds verification
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring (minimum every 6 months)
- Additional documentation

6.3 Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)
Low-risk customers (government departments, listed companies) may be subjected to simplified measures with proper approval.

SECTION 7: POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEPs)

7.1 PEP Identification
Systems must be in place to identify:
- Foreign PEPs (heads of state, senior politicians, judicial officers)
- Domestic PEPs
- Family members and close associates of PEPs

7.2 PEP Requirements
For all PEP relationships:
- Approval from Managing Director/CEO level required
- Source of wealth must be established
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring is mandatory
- Any significant transactions must be scrutinized

SECTION 8: PENALTIES AND COMPLIANCE

8.1 Non-Compliance Penalties
Violations of these directions may result in:
- Monetary penalty up to INR 1 crore (One Crore)
- Restrictions on business operations
- Criminal prosecution under PMLA 2002 with imprisonment up to 10 years

8.2 Compliance Certificate
Annual compliance certificate must be submitted to RBI by June 30 each year, certified by the Principal Officer and CEO.

8.3 Internal Audit
Internal audit must verify AML/CFT compliance at least annually, covering all branches and processes.
